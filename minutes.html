About the Group Business Meeting

The Group Business Meeting / Group Conscience is held during our regular meeting on the last day of each month at 5:00 a.m. for up to one (1) hour, as needed.

The Group Conscience is the result of the Group’s Business Meeting discussions. The Group Conscience is the will of the Group. The guiding principles for the Group Conscience are always the 12 Traditions and the 12 Concepts of Service (please see 2022-2025 Al-Anon / Alateen Service Manual, pp. 48-49).

Please join us!

All members are encouraged to share their knowledge, in order to make informed Group Conscience decisions. We also encourage a thorough discussion, hearing all views. Our Group uses the 2022-2025 Al-Anon / Alateen Service Manual as a guide to answer Group questions. When voting on Group Conscience motions, the Group uses a two-thirds (2/3rds) majority of yes to no votes. Abstentions are noted, but not included in the count.

The minutes on this site are for informational purposes only.

Members’ names are not included in this version of the minutes to maintain anonymity on this website.

To request a copy of the full business meeting minutes, please send an email to newday7minutes@gmail.com. In your email please include the date of the minutes you are requesting in the subject field.

Group Representative Report:

Information about the WSO can be found on the website Al-Anon. Org. The very first Global Electronic Area (GEA) Assembly meeting was held July 31, 2022. WSO information can be read in three languages now: Spanish, French and English. Our GEA delegate related his topics of discussion from the World Service Conference. Among these are recommendations for patience, time, work, and service with a focus on gratitude. The average age of Al-Anon members is 62. Our GR relayed three questions from our Area Chair who asked us to consider:

  1. What are your top 3 priorities for the area?
  2. How should districts be organized?
  3. What service positions* are you willing and able to contribute?

*Information about service positions can be found under Links of Service in our Service Manual.

To date no responses from our group have been received. The GR explained that her research consisted of reading responses on the GR website from other groups. Some of her own thoughts were mirrored.

  1. Electing an alternate delegate to the Area.
  2. Create an area website to make Area information and activities available to all members.
  3. As we continue form our area, reach out to other areas to help us understand what can and cannot work for our electronic area.

Thoughts on the formation of districts within our area include categorizing groups according to time zones and electronic platforms such as Zoom and telephone. There are currently 16 platforms and 800 registered groups in our area.

Our Chair and GR raised as an urgent priority an issue about voting, as this substantially affects how we run our business meetings. The Chair made a motion to discuss and vote on the way we format our votes. The need for consistency was stressed. The motion is to always take a yes or no vote of members present at the business meeting. We would no longer take a vote by asking whether anyone is opposed. This practice has been contested at every business meeting for the past several months. Once established, the motion for a yes or no vote will save our meeting precious time and avoid needless controversy. The motion was tabled for the next business meeting.

There was a call for a discussion about “dominance” in terms of the daily readings as well as possible wording for our meeting format.

At our September 2022 Business Meeting:

  1. Our first order of old business as a priority will be to ask for a second to the motion for a yes/no vote format, discuss the motion, vote on the yes/no motion.
  2. Our second order of old business will be to continue to discuss the calling of a group conscience, upon which there will be a motion, possible further discussion, and vote on this matter.
  3. Our third order of old business is to discuss use of any left-over time from our business meeting.
  4. Our fourth order of old business is a motion to discuss dominance of readers and possible wording for our meeting format.

The business meeting ended at 6:02am with the Serenity prayer.

Old business-Secretary read minutes from June 30, 2022 business meeting.

Treasurer report- Ending balance in June $427.26. Receipts in July, $35.00. Current balance of $462.26.

GR report- A Global Electronic Assembly Meeting will be held today. Our GR will attend and bring information back to our group.

Business chair read literature on Knowledge Based Decision Making vs Roberts Rules. Al-Anon currently uses the KBDM format.

First order of old business: Treasurer would like to bring to closure the $200 reserve amount. At the last business meeting, a motion was made and seconded for the $200 reserve amount. Now we need discussion and vote. Discussion was held and the vote was 18-yes, zero-no and zero-abstain. Treasurer discussed the percentage of group contributions in excess of the prudent reserve to be allocated to our service arms, the World Service Office (WSO) and the Global Electronic Area (GEA). The Service Manual suggests that our group contributions be in proportion to the services provided. The treasurer recommends at this time 70% to WSO and 30% to GEA, on a monthly basis. These percentages may change as our GEA develops. A motion was made, seconded, and voted: yes-13, no-zero, abstain-zero. The WSO announced that expenses per group for services provided are $283/group, also known as the annual equalized expense. Our goal is to contribute back to the WSO at least this amount to be self-supporting. Now that the group treasury is fully formed, our contributions support our Newcomers, WSO and Area. Our treasurer stated that contributions have been received at the rate of 2-3 per month since April. It is hoped that with the knowledge of where our group funds support, our members will feel comfortable to contribute directly to our family group.

Second order of old business: Literature chairperson discussion. This is not an anonymous position. Literature chair needs to supply their address and phone number. Also, literature chair needs to protect anonymity of person requesting packets. Our GR gave an overview of the position. Time commitment, 3 months, 6 months, a year was discussed. Also, rotation of service was discussed. Ellen volunteered for this position. and will try it for 6 months. The vote for Ellen was 10-yes, zero-no and zero-abstain.

Third order of old business: Use of any left-over time from our business meeting. Suggestions are 1. Discussion of tradition/concept of the month, 2. Daily readers, 3. Shares. The amount of time left over can help determine the activity. We can always rotate the activity. This item was tabled for further discussion at the next Business Meeting.

Fourth order of old business: The GR raised the question, when a member calls for a group conscience (that is, an additional business meeting), how do we address this need? The proposal is that a group conscience meeting request, due to the urgency and gravity of such a request, cannot be tabled or postponed but must be subject to an immediate vote for an additional meeting and its date and time. Discussion at the next business meeting should include the timing of the additional meeting (for example, 15 minutes before the close of the designated meeting). It is suggested that the additional meeting will take place after seven (7) days of announcing at daily meetings. An example of an exception to this rule would be an impending deadline within the seven-day window to meet a request from the WSO or GEA. This issue was tabled due to time constraints.

At the end of the meeting the business chair read the definition of Knowledge Based Decision Making (KBDM).

At our August 2022 Business Meeting:

  1. Our first order of old business will be to continue to discuss the calling of a group conscience, upon which there will be a motion, possible further discussion, and vote on this matter.

  2. Our second order of old business is to discuss use of any left-over time from our business meeting.

  3. Our third order of old business is to discuss our group’s decision making processs which invites all voices to be heard.

The Meeting closed with the Serenity Prayer at 6:04AM.
